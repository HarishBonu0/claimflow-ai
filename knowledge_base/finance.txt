COMPREHENSIVE FINANCIAL CONCEPTS - SIMPLE ENGLISH

PART 1: DEDUCTIBLES EXPLAINED IN DETAIL

What is Deductible?
Deductible is the money YOU pay first when you file a claim.
Insurance pays only AFTER you pay your deductible.
Think of it as your "share" of the damage.

Simple Example 1:
Your deductible = 500 rupees
Damage costs = 2000 rupees
YOU pay = 500 rupees (your deductible)
Insurance pays = 1500 rupees (full amount above deductible)
Total paid = 2000 rupees

Simple Example 2:
Deductible = 0 rupees (no deductible)
Damage = 2000 rupees
YOU pay = 0
Insurance pays = 2000 rupees

Simple Example 3:
Deductible = 5000 rupees
Damage = 3000 rupees
YOU pay = Full 3000 rupees! (damage less than deductible)
Insurance pays = 0 rupees
This happens when damage is less than deductible.

Deductible Strategy
Higher deductible = Lower monthly premium (you save money now)
Lower deductible = Higher monthly premium (you pay more monthly)
Most people choose = 500 to 1000 rupees

Choose based on:
- How much cash can you afford to pay?
- How often do you use insurance?
- Your financial situation?

PART 2: COVERAGE AND LIMITS

What is Coverage?
Coverage = What insurance will pay for
Insurance pays only for covered items.
If something is NOT covered, you pay 100%.

Example Coverages:
Health insurance covers: Doctor, hospital, surgery, medicines
Car insurance covers: Accidents, theft, damage, liability
Home insurance covers: Fire, theft, water damage, earthquake (if added)
Life insurance covers: Death benefit to family

What is Coverage Limit?
Coverage limit = Maximum amount insurance pays
Insurance will NOT pay more than the limit.
You must pay the rest.

Coverage Limit Example 1:
Your limit = 10,000 rupees
Damage cost = 15,000 rupees
Insurance pays = 10,000 rupees (stops at limit)
YOU pay = 5,000 rupees (the rest)

Coverage Limit Example 2:
Your limit = 50,000 rupees
Damage cost = 30,000 rupees
Insurance pays = 30,000 rupees (full, under limit)
YOU pay = 0 rupees

Types of Coverage Limits

1. Per-Incident Limit
Maximum for one accident/claim only.
Example: 50,000 limit per accident
Multiple accidents in year = Limit resets each time

2. Annual Limit
Maximum for entire year.
Example: 1 lakh limit per year
Use it up, insurance stops covering.
Resets next year.

3. Category Limit
Different limits for different types.
Example: Jewelry limit = 50000, Furniture limit = 100000
Helps keep premiums low.

PART 3: CO-INSURANCE AND CO-PAYMENT

What is Co-Insurance?
Co-insurance = You and insurance share the cost as percentage.
Not a fixed amount - it's a percentage.

Co-Insurance Example:
Insurance is 80%, You are 20%
Damage = 1000 rupees
Insurance pays = 80% = 800 rupees
YOU pay = 20% = 200 rupees

Insurance is 70%, You are 30%
Damage = 2000 rupees
Insurance pays = 70% = 1400 rupees
YOU pay = 30% = 600 rupees

What is Co-Payment?
Co-payment = Fixed amount you pay every time
Not percentage - always same amount.

Co-Payment Examples:
Doctor visit = You always pay 100 rupees
Hospital = You always pay 500 rupees
Medicine = You always pay 50 rupees
Rest = Insurance pays

Co-Payment in Health Insurance:
Common in health insurance.
Small amount per visit (50-500 rupees).
Insurance pays rest.
Helps share cost fairly.

Difference: Co-Insurance vs Co-Payment
Co-Insurance: Percentage (depends on amount)
Co-Payment: Fixed amount (always same)

Example:
Medical bill = 1000 rupees
With 20% co-insurance: You pay 200 rupees
With 100 rupees co-payment: You pay 100 rupees

PART 4: DETAILED SETTLEMENT CALCULATION

How Settlement Amount is Calculated

Step 1: Find Total Damage Cost
Get repair estimate (mechanic quote for car damage)
Get medical bills (for health claim)
Get police report for theft
Get photos of damage
Calculate total loss.

Step 2: Check Policy Terms
Is this damage covered? (Check coverage section)
What is your deductible? (Your share)
What is coverage limit? (Maximum insurance pays)
What is co-insurance percentage? (Your percentage share)

Step 3: Subtract Deductible
Damage cost - Your deductible = Remaining amount
Example: 5000 - 500 = 4500

Step 4: Apply Coverage Limit
Check if amount exceeds limit.
If yes, amount = limit
If no, use the amount

Example:
Remaining = 4500 rupees
Limit = 4000 rupees
Insurance pays = 4000 rupees (limited)

Step 5: Apply Co-Insurance If Any
If co-insurance applies, deduct your percentage.
Example: 80-20 split
Insurance pays 80% of final amount.
You pay 20%.

Step 6: Final Insurance Payment
Final amount = Money you receive

Complete Settlement Example:
Damage cost: 10,000 rupees
Your deductible: 1,000 rupees
After deductible: 9,000 rupees
Your coverage limit: 8,000 rupees
After limit: 8,000 rupees
Co-insurance: 80-20 (insurance 80%, you 20%)
Insurance pays: 8000 × 80% = 6,400 rupees
YOU pay: 8000 × 20% = 1,600 rupees

Summary:
You paid before claim: 1,000 rupees (deductible)
You paid from co-insurance: 1,600 rupees
Total YOU paid: 2,600 rupees
Insurance paid: 6,400 rupees
Total covered damage: 9,000 rupees (out of 10,000)

PART 5: PREMIUMS - MONTHLY PAYMENTS

What is Premium?
Premium = Money you pay monthly to insurance company
This is your "fee" for protection.
Pay premium, get coverage.
Stop premium, coverage stops!

Premium Depends On:
1. Type of insurance (health, car, home, life)
2. Deductible amount (higher = lower premium)
3. Coverage limits (higher = higher premium)
4. Your risk level (age, health, driving record, location)
5. Your past claims history (more claims = higher premium)
6. Duration of policy (longer term = lower premium sometimes)

Premium Examples:
Health insurance: 300-1000 rupees per month
Car insurance: 500-2000 rupees per month
Home insurance: 200-500 rupees per month
Life insurance: 100-500 rupees per month

Premium Payment Options
Monthly: Automatic deduction from bank (most common)
Quarterly: Every 3 months (4 payments per year)
Half-yearly: Every 6 months (2 payments per year)
Yearly: Once a year (one large payment)

Most people choose: Monthly automatic payment

CRITICAL: If You Miss Premium Payment
Payment due: Example, Feb 1st
You don't pay by Feb 1st.
Warning: Insurance sends notice.
Days 1-14: Grace period (you can still pay).
Days 15+: Policy lapses (coverage stops).
Claim filed after lapse: Insurance REFUSES payment!

Prevention:
1. Set calendar reminder
2. Use automatic bank transfer
3. Check payment date when buying policy
4. Keep receipt of payment
5. Monitor emails/SMS reminders from insurance

PART 6: BANKING BASICS FOR INSURANCE

What is a Bank Account?
Bank account = Safe place to keep money
Bank protects your money (security).
Bank pays you interest (small amount).
You can withdraw anytime (except fixed deposits).

Types of Bank Accounts
1. Savings Account
- Easy to use, no restrictions
- Interest: 3-4% per year
- Can deposit/withdraw anytime
- Use for emergency money

2. Current Account
- For business use
- No interest
- Unlimited transactions
- Monthly charges

3. Fixed Deposit Account
- Money locked for set time (1-5 years)
- Interest: 5-7% per year
- Cannot withdraw early (penalty if you do)
- Safe, guaranteed returns

Bank Services for Insurance
Premium payment: Auto-deduct from account
Claim money: Receive in account
Easy tracking: Monthly statements

PART 7: DIFFERENT TYPES OF FINANCIAL SUPPORT

Health Insurance Support
Doctor visit co-payment: You pay 50-500 rupees
Hospital room charges: Insurance covers based on limit
Medicine: Insurance pays percentage (50-75%)
Surgery: Insurance covers most costs
Diagnostic tests: Insurance pays based on limit

Car Insurance Support
Repair costs: 100% covered up to limit
Deductible: You pay this amount
Total loss: Insurance pays car value
Third-party liability: Insurance pays to other person
Rental car while repair: Some policies cover

Home Insurance Support
Repair costs for damage: Covered
Furniture replacement: Covered up to category limit
Jewelry: Covered but low limit
Garden/landscape: Usually covered
Rental costs if house uninhabitable: Some policies cover

Life Insurance Support
Death benefit to family: Full amount
Accidental death: May pay double (varies by policy)
Suicide (first 1-2 years): Usually NOT covered
Beneficiary receives: Lump sum payment

PART 8: SUBROGATION - GETTING MONEY BACK

What is Subrogation?
Subrogation = Insurance gets money back from person who caused damage.
This helps keep insurance costs low for everyone.

Subrogation Example 1:
Car accident scenario:
Your car parked = Someone else's car hits it
Damage = 50,000 rupees
You claim = Insurance pays you 50,000 rupees
Insurance then sues = Gets 50,000 rupees from other driver's insurance
Result: Your insurance recovers their payment
Your cost: Only deductible (500-1000 rupees)

Subrogation Example 2:
Home damage from neighbor's construction:
Your home damaged = 2 lakhs rupees
You claim = Insurance pays you 1.8 lakhs (minus deductible)
Insurance sues neighbor = Gets 1.8 lakhs from neighbor's liability insurance
Result: You pay only your deductible
Neighbor's insurance pays rest

Subrogation Example 3:
Medical claim:
You injured by third party
Hospital bill = 1 lakh rupees
Insurance pays = 1 lakh
Insurance can sue third party = Get reimbursement
You don't do anything = Insurance handles

Why Subrogation is Important:
Keeps insurance affordable (costs stay low)
Insurance recovers money (sustainable business)
You pay less (only your part)
Wrongdoer pays (justice)
Fair system (each pays for their damage)

PART 9: INTEREST RATES AND RETURNS BASICS

What is Interest Rate?
Interest = Extra money lender gives you or takes from you
Bank savings = You earn interest (bank pays you)
Bank loan = You pay interest (you pay bank)

Interest Examples
Savings account: 3-4% per year
Fixed deposit: 5-7% per year
Bank loan: 8-12% per year
Credit card: 24-36% per year (expensive!)

How Interest Works

Savings Interest Example:
You deposit: 10,000 rupees
Interest rate: 10% per year
After 1 year: 10,000 + 1,000 = 11,000 rupees
You earned: 1,000 rupees (interest)

Loan Interest Example:
You borrow: 1,00,000 rupees
Interest rate: 10% per year
You must pay: 1,00,000 + 10,000 = 1,10,000 rupees
Your cost: 10,000 rupees (interest)

Compound Interest - Money Growing Faster
Initial amount: 10,000 rupees
Interest rate: 10% per year
Year 1: 10,000 × 1.10 = 11,000 rupees
Year 2: 11,000 × 1.10 = 12,100 rupees
Year 3: 12,100 × 1.10 = 13,310 rupees
Total after 3 years = 13,310 rupees (earned 3,310 rupees)

Benefits: Money grows faster as time increases!

PART 10: INFLATION AND PURCHASING POWER

What is Inflation?
Inflation = General increase in prices from year to year
Today's rupee becomes less valuable tomorrow.

Inflation Example:
Item: 1 kg rice
2020: Costs 25 rupees
2023: Costs 35 rupees
Inflation: Prices went up 40% in 3 years

Impact on You:
Your salary stays same = You can buy less
Your savings lose value = 10,000 today ≠ 10,000 in 5 years
Need to earn more = To maintain same lifestyle

Fighting Inflation:
Save in FD (gets interest to beat inflation)
Invest in mutual funds (potential growth)
Increase skills = Earn more money
Adjust spending = Use money wisely
